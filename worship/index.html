Worship at LCR

Worship connects people with God, who brings healing, hope, and wholeness through the life, death, and resurrection of Jesus Christ.

In Christ's church you matter and everyone is welcome here, regardless of race, sexual orientation or age.

Worship and Holy Communion

Worship at LCR blends contemporary and traditional expressions. Come as you are. Whether you are new to church, simply curious, or a maturing disciple, there is a place for you here.

Holy Communion is central to worship. The bread and wine carry God's promise, given for all. That promise comes to us regardless of our membership or belief, regardless of our age, actions, or understanding, and it draws us into a deeper relationship with God. If the Spirit is inviting you to participate, you are encouraged to do just that.

Worship for daily life

Healing, hope, and Scripture

In worship God gathers us, feeds us, teaches us, and then sends us out to share his grace. Messages are rooted in Scripture and speak to daily life.

LCR sees science and other resources of life as partners in the search for understanding and insight into God's creation. You matter here, and everyone is welcome, regardless of race, sexual orientation or age.

Online worship

Watch a service

If you cannot be here in person, worship still comes to you. Services stream live on LCR's Facebook page, and you can watch them there afterward at a time that works for you.

Through the church year

Seasons of worship

Worship follows the rhythm of the church year. In seasons like Advent, the Saturday evening service takes on a quieter, more reflective feel, with prayer, meditation on Scripture, and song, and Holy Communion is celebrated.

Sermon notes and messages from LCR's worship life are gathered on the Messages and Music page.
