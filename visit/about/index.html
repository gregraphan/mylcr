About LCR

About LCR

Lutheran Church of the Resurrection is a faith community of the Evangelical Lutheran Church in America, celebrating creation's diversity as it is united in Christ.

Centered in Christ. Growing in faith. Joining God in the world.

Join Us for Worship

Identity

A Lutheran faith community

All people matter to God and to us. This church belongs to Christ, and we live as partners accountable to him. We are an ELCA congregation deeply rooted in grace, Word, and sacrament.

Lutherans are known for asking, "What does this mean?" In the ELCA, the word evangelical is used in its biblical sense: people who live out the gospel in word and deed, sharing the good news that has grabbed hold of our lives in every way possible, just by the way we live.

Mission

Centered, growing, and joining God's work

At the center of our life together is worship, where God gathers, feeds, teaches, and sends us to share grace.

"Our faith is a busy, living, active, mighty thing," wrote Martin Luther. We seek to draw closer to Christ through the power of the Holy Spirit. We are in the faith formation business, not to gain more members, but to know Christ crucified and risen.

Centered in Christ. Growing in faith. Joining God in the world.

Our life together

Rooted in the Reformation, living it today

"As I have loved you, so you must love one another. By this everyone will know that you are my disciples, if you love one another." These words from John's gospel shape how LCR tries to live.

LCR's roots run back to the Reformation. On October 31, 1517, Martin Luther posted ninety-five questions for scholarly debate on the church door in Wittenberg, Germany, and that call to keep asking what the gospel means still shapes Lutheran worship and life. God uses our hearts, hands, and voices to serve, restore, and reconcile the world. God's work. Our hands.
